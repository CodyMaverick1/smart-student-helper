# Smart Study Helper

Building AI course project

## Summary

Smart Study Helper is an AI tool that helps students plan better study schedules. It looks at assignments, test dates, and study habits to suggest what to study and when.

## Background

Many students struggle with time management, especially when they have multiple classes, homework, exams, and projects at the same time. This problem is common in high school and college. My motivation is that students often know they need to study, but they do not always know what to focus on first.

This topic is important because better planning can lower stress and help students perform better in school.

## How is it used?

A student would enter their classes, assignments, due dates, exam dates, and how much time they have each day. The AI would then suggest a study plan. For example, if a test is coming soon and the student has not studied much, the system would move that subject higher on the schedule.

The main users would be students. Teachers or advisors could also use it to help students who are falling behind.

## Data sources and AI methods

The project would use data entered by the student, such as due dates, grades, class difficulty, and study time. It could also use past study results to learn what works best for the student.

Helpful AI methods could include prediction models and recommendation systems. The AI could predict which assignments need the most attention and recommend a study schedule.

## Challenges

This project would not solve every problem with studying. It cannot force students to actually do the work. It also might make bad suggestions if the student enters wrong information. Privacy is important because school data and grades can be personal.

## What next?

This project could grow into a full app with calendar reminders, progress tracking, and study tips. It could also connect to school websites like Canvas or Google Calendar. To improve it, I would need help with app design, data privacy, and testing it with real students.

## Acknowledgments

This idea was inspired by students who struggle with planning schoolwork and by recommendation systems used in apps like calendars, shopping websites, and study tools.
